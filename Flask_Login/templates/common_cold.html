You have: Common Cold

Description:

The common cold is a mild viral infection of the upper respiratory tract, mainly affecting the nose and throat. It’s very common, especially in fall and winter months, and usually not serious, but it can be very annoying!

Symptoms:

  • Runny nose
  • Sneezing
  • Sore throat
  • Cough
  • Mild fever
  • Fatigue

Causes:

  • Rhinoviruses (most common)
  • Coronaviruses
  • Respiratory syncytial virus (RSV)
  • Adenoviruses

Treatment:

Pain relievers (for aches and fever) Acetaminophen (Tylenol) Ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin) Decongestants (for stuffy nose) Pseudoephedrine (Sudafed) — oral Oxymetazoline (Afrin) — nasal spray (short-term use only, ≤3 days) Antihistamines (for runny nose/sneezing) Diphenhydramine (Benadryl) – causes drowsiness Loratadine (Claritin), Cetirizine (Zyrtec) – non-drowsy options Cough suppressants Dextromethorphan (Robitussin DM) Expectorants (to thin mucus) Guaifenesin (Mucinex) ⚠️ Antibiotics are not effective against viral infections like the common cold.

Honey – soothes cough and sore throat (especially effective at night) Saltwater gargle – relieves sore throat Steam inhalation – loosens congestion (can add eucalyptus oil) Warm fluids – tea, broth, warm lemon water to soothe throat and stay hydrated Vitamin C – might slightly reduce cold duration if taken regularly Zinc lozenges – may shorten the length of a cold if started early Elderberry syrup – potential antiviral and immune-boosting effects Rest – your body heals faster when rested Humidifier – moistens dry air to relieve nasal congestion and coughing

For more detailed information, consult your healthcare provider.
